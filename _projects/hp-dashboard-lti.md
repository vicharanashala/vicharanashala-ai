*Where progress is visible and learning stays accountable,*  
*HP turns every milestone into a step toward self-regulation.*

Developed at the Vicharanashala Lab, the HP Dashboard is a gamified learning and analytics system designed to strengthen self-regulated learning through visible progress, point-based accountability, and structured feedback. With LTI integration, the dashboard can connect to external learning platforms and course environments so that engagement tracking, milestones, and learner feedback remain part of a unified instructional experience.

---

## **About**

HP Dashboard is a learner-centered progress system built to move beyond static reporting. It combines learning analytics, gamified motivation, and structured reflection so that learners do not just see what they have done, but also understand what they should do next.

At the core of the platform is the Health Point (HP) system, which assigns visible value to effort, consistency, and completion. When integrated through LTI, the dashboard can sit inside a course ecosystem and remain connected to the learner’s ongoing academic experience.

---

## **The Challenge**

Digital learning environments often provide data without direction. Students may know their completion status, but they are rarely given a clear framework for planning effort, maintaining consistency, or reflecting on setbacks. This creates a gap between visibility and action.

The challenge is not just to track activity, but to design a system that supports SRL in a way that is motivating, practical, and easy to integrate into real courses. The platform must also connect smoothly with existing LMS workflows so that adoption does not become fragmented or disruptive.

---

## **The Platform**

HP Dashboard addresses this challenge through a structured motivation engine built around points, milestones, analytics, and integration support.

**Health Point System**

Learners earn HP when they complete assigned tasks, meet milestones, and maintain steady progress. Points make effort visible and give learners a concrete sense of advancement across the course.

**SRL Support**

The dashboard is designed around the three phases of self-regulated learning:

- **Forethought**: learners can view milestones, expected effort, and point targets before starting.
- **Performance**: real-time progress indicators and activity logs help learners monitor how they are doing during the task.
- **Self-Reflection**: feedback, point adjustments, and progress trends encourage learners to review habits and make corrections.

**LTI Integration**

Learning Tools Interoperability allows the dashboard to connect with external learning platforms and course environments in a standard way. This makes it possible to embed the HP experience within an LMS, align it with course activities, and keep learner data connected to the broader instructional journey.

LTI helps the dashboard function as part of the learning process rather than as a separate layer.

**Learning Analytics**

The system presents progress in a way that is easy for both learners and instructors to interpret. Visibility into engagement trends, task completion, and point movement helps identify where support is needed and where learners are building consistency.

| SRL Phase | Dashboard Support Feature | HP System Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Forethought | Milestone setting and goal tracking | Establishes point-based targets for planning |
| Performance | Real-time dashboards and activity logs | Provides immediate feedback on current engagement |
| Self-Reflection | AI-driven feedback and HP audits | Reinforces course correction and reflective practice |

## **The Impact**

HP Dashboard is designed to support sustained engagement rather than rushed, last-minute activity.

- Learners can track progress with clarity and accountability
- Instructors can monitor engagement patterns more effectively
- Course activity can remain connected through LTI-based integration
- The system encourages steady effort instead of binge-learning behavior

By making progress visible and consequences meaningful, the dashboard supports a healthier academic rhythm across the term.
