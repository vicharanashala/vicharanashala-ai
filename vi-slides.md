*Where curiosity leads and teaching follows,*  
*Vi-SlideS turns every question into the path learning follows.*

Developed at the Vicharanashala Lab, Indian Institute of Technology Ropar, Vi-SlideS is an AI-assisted, question-driven teaching support system designed to transform traditional lectures into adaptive and student-centered learning experiences. By integrating simple digital tools with intelligent processing, the platform ensures that student curiosity becomes the driving force of classroom interaction.

---

## **About**

Vi-SlideS is a question-driven learning system that transforms student queries into structured instructional input. After a brief topic introduction, students submit their questions through a digital interface, which are then automatically refined, organized, and presented to the instructor.

Instead of relying solely on pre-planned content, Vi-SlideS enables teaching to evolve dynamically based on what students actually think, ask, and struggle with. It creates a classroom environment where learning is guided by curiosity, not just content delivery.

---

## **The Challenge**

In both physical and online classrooms, many student questions remain unasked, unheard, or unaddressed.  
*How can teaching adapt in real time to what students truly want to understand?*

Traditional classroom interaction faces several barriers:
- Students hesitate to ask questions due to fear of judgment  
- Limited time prevents meaningful participation  
- Large classes lead to chat overload or ignored queries  
- Instructors struggle to manage multiple questions simultaneously  

As a result, teaching often remains lecture-driven rather than responsive to student needs.

The core challenge is not just enabling questions—but **capturing, organizing, and transforming them into actionable teaching input in real time**.

---

## **The Platform**

Vi-SlideS addresses this challenge through an AI-assisted pipeline that converts student questions into structured, presentation-ready content for adaptive teaching.

**Question Collection**

After a 5–10 minute topic introduction, students submit their questions through a simple interface such as Google Forms. This allows inclusive participation, with options for anonymity to encourage honest and uninhibited questioning.

**AI-Assisted Processing**

Once collected, questions are processed through an automated pipeline:

- Questions are refined for clarity while preserving original intent  
- Similar questions are grouped together to reduce redundancy  
- Key themes and areas of confusion are identified  

This transforms raw, unstructured inputs into meaningful insights that instructors can act upon immediately.

**Structured Slide Generation**

Each processed question or cluster is automatically converted into a slide. This creates a structured presentation where:

- Every slide represents a student concern  
- Questions are organized logically  
- No manual slide preparation is required  

Teaching becomes both structured and adaptive at the same time.

**Instructor-Led Discussion**

Instructors use the generated slides to guide the rest of the session. They address questions, clarify misconceptions, and adjust explanations based on student needs.

AI organizes—but the instructor leads.

**Adaptive Teaching**

As the session progresses, teaching dynamically evolves based on:
- Student questions  
- Identified knowledge gaps  
- Emerging themes  

This creates a responsive learning environment where instruction aligns with real-time understanding.
