## ViBe
*Like Vikram with Betaal at every stride,*
*ViBe questions you—till trust and confidence walk by your side.*


Officially launched on 29th July 2025 by Honourable Education Minister Shri Dharmendra Pradhan at the Akhil Bharatiya Shiksha Samagam, ViBe is an open-source platform released under the MIT License. It remains free and is actively sustained by a collaborative developer and academic community committed to ensuring trust and confidence in the online learning process. You can explore the codebase and contribute to the movement by visiting our [GitHub Repository](https://github.com/vicharanashala/vibe).

**About**

ViBe is a digital space dedicated to transforming passive observation into genuine cognitive engagement. The platform brings together a structure learning environment for learners, automated insights for educators, and an instructional design explicitly built to ensure learners trust the process and feel confident in what they are learning. Discover the philosophy behind this transformation by watching our [Concept Video](https://youtu.be/6AYCVKDIeTs?si=uTpsOaHkDvGf53sQ).

**The Challenge**

While digital learning has solved the problem of access, completion rates for self-paced video courses consistently hover around 10 to 15 percent globally. Most platforms simply deliver content and assume learning automatically follows. Without a human mentor's gaze, learners often slip into passive viewing without active processing, leading to unverified learning outcomes and surface-level compliance. The core challenge is designing a framework that balances behavioral accountability with intrinsic motivation, ensuring that monitoring builds trust rather than anxiety.

**The Platform**

Inspired by the ancient riddles of Betaal to King Vikramaditya—where every faltered answer demands a backward glance—ViBe acts as a digital scaffold anchored in an integrated, three-theory learning science framework. You can experience these features firsthand through our [Interactive Demo](https://youtu.be/8ytNdYlK-BU?si=tYHqwjnJjAQx85C9).

**Micro-Learning**
Long lectures are automatically segmented at conceptual breakpoints. By consolidating each concept before the next arrives, ViBe manages working memory, reduces extraneous cognitive load, and prevents learner overload, directly applying Sweller's Cognitive Load Theory.

**Smart Assessments**
Active testing events and quizzes are inserted directly into the viewing flow rather than reserved for massed end-of-module review. Testing memory actively during learning strengthens retention significantly more than passive re-reading or delayed assessments, a principle grounded in Roediger and Karpicke's Retrieval Practice Theory.

**Active Verification**
AI-driven proctoring monitors focus signals, such as tab switches and absence from the camera, in real-time. By carefully balancing this behavioral accountability with the learner's psychological need for autonomy, ViBe is designed to foster deep cognitive engagement and trust, rather than mere surface compliance, addressing the dynamics outlined in Deci and Ryan's Self-Determination Theory.

**ViBeCrowd**
Within this collaborative ecosystem, learners do not just consume content but actively curate, refine, and validate questions. This builds a self-improving knowledge base that further reinforces genuine cognitive engagement.

**The Impact**

Deployed nationally under the Ministry of Education’s Malaviya Mission Teacher Training Programme, ViBe has engaged over 3,500 learners for more than 23,000 verified watch hours. It has demonstrated a 50 percent increase in course completion rates and reduced operational costs by 60 percent, delivering verified skill acquisition at a population scale of approximately ₹7 per learner per hour.
